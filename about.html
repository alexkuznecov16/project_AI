What is it?

Artificial intelligence (AI) is intelligence demonstrated by machines, as opposed to intelligence of humans and other animals. Example tasks in which this is done include speech recognition, computer vision, translation between (natural) languages, as well as other mappings of inputs.

Who is author?

Officially published in 1956 at a summer seminar at Dartmouth College (Hanover, USA), which was organized by four American scientists: John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester and Claude Shannon.

What are the tasks?

The main tasks of artificial intelligence are: analysis and problem solving, the possibility of self-learning, the ability to perceive, reproduce human speech, move and plan movement parameters, etc.

What is the benefit?

AI-based technologies help improve work efficiency and productivity by automating processes and tasks that were previously performed by humans. AI is also able to interpret volumes of data that a human cannot interpret.

What is the problems?

The problem of controlling artificial intelligence is a challenge in artificial intelligence (AI) engineering and philosophy. It consists in creating an artificial superintelligence that will be useful to humans, while avoiding the unintentional creation of a superintelligence that will cause harm.
